Sour Soup

Introdution: "Sour soup," or "canh chua" in Vietnamese, is a delightful dish with a balance of sweet and sour flavors.

Note: The image is for illustration purposes only and may not represent the exact dish.

Key Ingredients

  • Sour Agent
    • Tamarind: This is a very common source of the sour flavor.
    • Pineapple: Adds both sweetness and a tangy flavor.
    • Tomatoes: Contribute to the sourness and add a rich, red color.
  • Protein
    • fish
    • Shrimp
  • Vegetables
    • Okra: Adds a unique texture and flavor.
    • Bean sprouts
    • Water spinach: A popular leafy green in Vietnamese cuisine.
  • Aromatic and herbs
    • Garlic
    • Shallots
    • Cilantro
    • Thai basil
  • Seasonings
    • Fish sauces
    • Sugar
    • Salt
    • Black pepper
    • Chili pepper

General Recipe Steps

  1. Prepare the Ingredients
    • Clean and cut the protein into bite-sized pieces.
    • Prepare the vegetables: slice the okra, dice the tomatoes and pineapple, etc.
    • Prepare the tamarind: If using fresh tamarind, soak it in warm water and extract the juice. If using tamarind paste, dilute it with water.
    • Mince the garlic and shallots.
  2. Sauté the Aromatics
    • Heat oil in a pot and sauté the garlic and shallots until fragrant.
  3. Add the Protein
    • Add the protein to the pot and stir-fry until it is cooked through.
  4. Add the Sour Agent and Vegetables
    • Pour in the tamarind juice or add the pineapple and tomatoes.
    • Add the okra and any other vegetables you are using.
    • Let it simmer for a few minutes.
  5. Season the Soup
    • Season with fish sauce, sugar, salt, and chili peppers to taste.
  6. Garnish and Serve
    • Garnish with fresh herbs like cilantro and Thai basil.
    • Serve hot with steamed rice or noodles.

And that is how you can make your own Sour Soup!
